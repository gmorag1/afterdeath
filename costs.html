What Things Actually Cost

Traditional Funeral + Burial

$8,300 – $16,000

Includes viewing, ceremony, casket, embalming, hearse, burial plot, and headstone. The national median is $8,300 for the service; cemetery costs add $4,000–$8,000.

Cremation with Service

$4,000 – $7,000

Includes a memorial ceremony, cremation, and an urn. Growing in popularity — over 60% of Californians now choose cremation.

Direct Cremation

$1,500 – $3,000

The most affordable option. No viewing or ceremony. The body is cremated, and ashes are returned to the family. A memorial can be held later at any time.

Flowers

$200 – $1,500

Sympathy bouquets start around $75. Standing sprays and casket pieces range from $200–$500+. Total floral costs depend on how many arrangements are ordered.

Catering / Reception

$500 – $5,000

Post-service gatherings range from simple deli trays ($8–15/person) to full catering ($25–50/person). For 50 guests, budget $500–$2,500.

Estate & Probate Attorney

$3,000 – $15,000+

California probate costs 4–7% of estate value. A $500,000 estate: $13,000+ in legal and executor fees. Trusts avoid probate entirely.

Death Certificates

$100 – $350

California charges ~$21 for the first certified copy and ~$6 for each additional. Order 10–15 copies.

Obituary

$0 – $600

Free on social media and some online platforms. Newspaper obituaries (SF Chronicle) cost $300–$600+.

You Have Options

Financial Help Is Available

You don't have to figure this out alone. Here are real options that help thousands of families every year.

🏛️

Funeral Home Payment Plans

Many funeral homes offer interest-free payment plans that spread costs over 6–12 months. Always ask — most don't advertise this. Under the FTC Funeral Rule, funeral homes are required to give you an itemized price list. You have the right to choose only the services you want.

🏛️

Government Assistance

FEMA Funeral Assistance: If the death was COVID-related, FEMA may reimburse up to $9,000 in funeral expenses. Social Security Death Benefit: A one-time $255 payment for surviving spouses. County Indigent Burial Programs: San Francisco, Alameda, and other Bay Area counties offer assistance for families who cannot afford funeral costs.

FEMA: 844-684-6333

🎖️

Veteran Benefits

The VA provides: burial allowance up to $2,000+ (service-connected death) or $948 (non-service-connected), free burial in a national cemetery (Golden Gate National Cemetery in San Bruno), free government headstone or marker, burial flag, and Presidential Memorial Certificate.

VA: 1-800-827-1000

💚

Community Fundraising

GoFundMe funeral campaigns raise an average of $4,500 and are among the most supported campaign types. Be specific about costs, share on social media, and update donors. There is no shame in asking your community for help during the hardest time of your life.

Know Your Rights

  • ✓ Under the FTC Funeral Rule, funeral homes must provide an itemized General Price List.
  • ✓ You are not required to purchase a casket from the funeral home — you can buy one elsewhere.
  • ✓ You can decline embalming in most cases.
  • ✓ You have the right to choose only the services you want.
  • ✓ Never feel pressured to spend more than you can afford — your loved one would not want that.
